The Core: A Haven You Can Call Your Own

      Merriam-Webster defines the ‘core’ as, “a basic, essential, or enduring part (as of an individual, a class, or an entity).” The word is often used in astronomy, in health and fitness (i.e. yoga) and in philosophy. It’s also thrown around on a regular basis in conversations pertaining to the heart of something or someone. Yet somehow, it all leads back to describing one similar thing—the most intimate, essential and foundational part. This principle is the guiding force that motivated the founders to build The Core.
      The Core is a multi-faceted space that aspire to inspire passionate souls—a space where one can express themselves freely and uniquely, a space that welcomes all walks of life who yearn to physically manifest their inner core whether they may be a performer, a fitness instructor, a craftsman, an art exhibitor or plainly, someone looking for a good time.
      “Everything in life needs us to develop our inner core to become better individuals. Whether our purpose is for recreation or for our profession, our strength is centered at ourselves, in our hearts, in our core,” says Margarita (Margo) D. Frasco, the woman behind MDF Productions, the creator of The Core. The idea stems from her personal experience of directing a theatrical play a few years back. One of the major obstacles of the production at the time was finding a place to hold their rehearsals. From then on, she realized that this was a shared problem among independent theater productions in the city. And as her network of freelance performers, artists and instructors grew, she witnessed first hand that there was a demand for a space that could cater their needs as well. “I thought, ‘How wonderful would it be to have your own space where you’re free to be yourself and release your energy,’” she said.
      With her insight of making The Core comfortable and accommodating to everyone who enters and utilizes the space, she also wanted to impart a piece of her home to it by adding yellow warm lights in its interiors. The Core’s flooring are basket weave sprung to provide safety for all types of physical activity. Its black walls and curtains that are able to cover their mirrors aim to transform a rehearsal studio into an intimate area for seminars, conferences, workshops, discussions and/or exhibits. And to ensure that The Core is open for all, it holds a very competitive yet affordable price.
      Being the first of its kind in Cebu, The Core by MDF Productions still seeks to open more branches and to provide more multi-faceted community spaces all over the city. Advocating for pure Cebuano talents, she plans on holding pocket events every two months within the studio. Margo also aims to start theatrical productions through MDF Productions in the near future.
